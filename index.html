co-op games

In 2024 I started playing co-op games with my friends like Lethal Company and I started to look for other co-op games. It got to a point where I was telling my friends about so many co-op games they eventually said: "Make a website, we can't keep track." So I did. Along the way I user-tested and found that one of the things people didn't like was that the theme didn't match the audience; so I added a dark mode toggle and set it to dark by default, I also changed the cards to pink instead of dark blue to make them pop. (most images aren't my work in this project)

movies

Since 2022 I've kept a document on my phone where I've added movies I've watched, because my family was always asking me for movie suggestions. Eventually I couldn't keep up with maintanence of the doc so I thought it could be a website where I can show how good I think the movie is, what tags I gave it and if I recommend it. I haven't stopped getting feedback and making changes; recently I changed the cards from being either red, yellow, or green to, instead, being a color based on how much I recommend it based on an algorithm. What sets this project apart from my others is I made a database and the website is grabbing data through an api. This will allow me to keep it maintained and updated easily, unlike my co-op games website. (most images aren't my work in this project)

check it out here:

https://batterydrain.github.io/movies/

SCRIPTLESS

I conducted a survey in class asking people about their public speaking skils 40% of respondents (45 in total) said they were either not confident or somewhat not confident. Despite this 96% of them publicly spoke frequently or more. This means that a large portion are leaving bad impressions when forced to publicly speak. When asked how people prepared the most common answer by far was people wrote a script. SCRIPTLESS was born. A web app to help you write, remember and practice your scripts.

check it out here:

https://interactivedesign.ca/~bwebster/
